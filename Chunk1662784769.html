Communications

Task: 236007

Static Wicks.
  1. Inspect wicks for condition and security. If wick shows signs of deterioration, replace. If wick shows signs of lightning strike, replace wick and inspect entire aircraft for lighting strike damage in accordance with the 208 Series Maintenance Manual Chapter 5-50.
    Note:

    Wick type discharges are functional as long as any material is present at the tip and their resistance is within the range of 1 to 100 megohms. If you have doubts as to the effectiveness of a wick, perform the high resistance check as outlined in the 208 Series Maintenance Manual, Chapter 23-60.

  2. Inspect static wick mount base for condition, cracks and security. Pay particular attention to the area highlighted in Detail A below.

Task: 236011

SPECIAL TOOLS
  • Digital ohmmeter.
  • Megohmmeter.
Static Discharger Test.
  1. Perform high resistance check in accordance with the following procedure.
    1. With ohmmeter, check resistance from discharger base to a good airplane ground. Any resistance greater than 0.50 ohm indicates a poor ground. Establish a good ground before going to the next step.
    2. With discharger mounted on airplane, connect EARTH terminal on Megger tester to base of discharger. Using the 500-volt scale of Megger, check resistance at tip of discharger. If the 500-volt scale reads less than 1 megohm or greater than 100 megohms, replace discharger.
Note:

For additional information, refer to the 208 Series Maintenance Manual, Chapter 23-60.
