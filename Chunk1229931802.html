Zone Inspection

TASK: 051014

Dry Wing (Left and Right) Zonal Inspection (Zones 511, 611, 501, 601, 503, 603, 523, 623, 551, 651, 526, and 625).
Note:

This zones encompassed in this task are for the dry portions of the wings only. Do Not open the wet portions of the wings unless an unairworthy condition is suspected. Refer to section 06-2 for further zone identification.

Note:

Remove access panels as necessary for inspection.

  1. Inspect accessible electrical wiring, ground straps, and connectors for condition, routing, proper support, chafing, signs of overheating, security of connectors, and condition of hardware.

    Pay particular attention to wiring where it passes rib cutouts and in wing root area where wiring harness enters fuselage.

  2. Inspect all accessible tubing, lines, and ducts for condition, chafing, evidence of damage, leaks, and security.
  3. Inspect all push/pull cable assemblies for condition, wear, cracks and security.
  4. Inspect all bracketry, fittings, and clamps associated with wiring, tubing, lines, ducts, and push/pull cables for condition, corrosion, and security.
  5. Visually inspect accessible spar, ribs, stringers, skin, and removed panels for condition, cracks dents, and corrosion.
  6. Inspect corrosion preventive coating for condition, flaking, peeling and scratches.

    All 208 aircraft are manufactured with an epoxy primer or corrosion proofing on all surfaces of structural components. This coating acts as a corrosion barrier and must be maintained to obtain the full benefits from this process. Any exposed metal from damaged protective coatings should be treated for corrosion and re-apply protective coatings as soon as possible in accordance with the 208 Series Maintenance Manual, Ch. 20-31

TASK COMPLETE
